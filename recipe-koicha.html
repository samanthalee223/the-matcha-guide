Koicha / Thick Tea

Koicha is a traditional way of preparing matcha with double the matcha powder and half of the water used for usucha to create a rich, thick paste. Koicha is traditionally prepared as a communal drink to be shared amongst guests. It is prepared with the highest quality matcha as it is the best way to appreciate any matcha powder’s unfiltered flavours. Discover More About Koicha!

Materials and Ingredients:

  • 2g or 1/2 teaspoon of matcha
  • 30mL of hot water (80°C or 175°F is recommended)
  • Chasen/bamboo whisk
  • Chawan (tea bowl)
  • Chashaku (tea spoon) or measuring spoon
  • Tea sifter
  • Measuring scale

Koicha requires the highest quality matcha and the traditional tea ceremony utensils. Alternatives to the Chasen whisk are not recommended for this recipe.

Instructions

1. Soak your whisk in hot water for 30-60 seconds. Be sure to wet the top of the tines as well.

2. Measure out 4g or 1 teaspoon of matcha and sift it into the bowl.

3. Add 30mL of hot water (80°C), just enough to cover the powder

4. Stir slowly for 15 seconds in a circular motion and turn into a paste

5. Enjoy by itself or as a topping for desserts like ice cream to make matcha affogato! You can also make usucha with the leftover matcha in the bowl.

Finished your matcha? Make sure to clean up properly!
