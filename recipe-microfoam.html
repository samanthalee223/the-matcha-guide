30-20-10 Microfoam Usucha

Usucha is the most common way to drink matcha and is a great way to discover a matcha powder’s flavour profile. Japanese tea ceremony schools have different standards for foam on usucha, so the amount of foam in your matcha depends on personal preference. The 30-20-10 technique is an effective way to get a fine layer of foam and provides a creamier consistency. This whisking technique can also be used for latte ratios.

Materials and Ingredients:

  • 4g or 1 teaspoon of matcha
  • 50-100mL of hot water (80°C or 175°F is recommended for the matcha to foam properly)
  • Chasen/bamboo whisk (recommended) or an electric frother
  • Chawan (tea bowl)
  • Chashaku (tea spoon) or measuring spoon
  • Tea sifter
  • Measuring scale

You do not need all of the tea ceremony utensils to make a good bowl of matcha, but they are recommended! You can also change the matcha to water ratios according to personal taste or matcha quality.

Instructions

1. Soak your whisk in hot water for 30-60 seconds. Be sure to wet the top of the tines as well.

2. Measure out 4g or 1 teaspoon of matcha and sift it into the bowl.

3. Add 90mL of hot water (80°C) or adjust according to preference

4. Whisk vigorously in a zig-zag motion for 30 seconds to make large air bubbles. Keep your whisk close to the bottom of the bowl but not touching it, using mainly your wrist rather than your arm.

5. Slightly lift the whisk and continue whisking in the zig-zag motion for 20 seconds at a slightly slower pace to pop the larger air bubbles.

6. Slowly and gently glide the whisk for 10 seconds near the surface to pop the rest of the bubbles and create a microfoam

7. Enjoy as Usucha or add any additional ingredients such as milk or sweetener according to preference!

Finished your matcha? Make sure to clean up properly!
