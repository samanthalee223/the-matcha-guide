Matcha Latte

Hot matcha lattes are the most beginner friendly matcha drink as it is smooth, sweet, and easy for a daily drink. This is also a good option if you do not have a matcha that isn’t high enough quality for usucha.

Materials and Ingredients:

  • 3g of matcha
  • 30-40mL of hot water (80°C or 175°F is recommended)
  • 90-120mL of your warmed or foamed milk of choice
  • Optional: 1tbsp of your sweetener of choice (honey, sugar, agave syrup, brown sugar syrup, etc.)
  • Chasen/bamboo whisk (recommended) or an electric frother
  • Chawan (tea bowl)
  • Chashaku (tea spoon) or measuring spoon
  • Tea sifter
  • Measuring scale

You do not need all of the tea ceremony utensils to make a good bowl of matcha, but they are recommended! You can also change the matcha to water and milk ratios according to personal taste.

Instructions

1. Soak your whisk in hot water for 30-60 seconds. Be sure to wet the top of the tines as well.

2. Measure out 3g of matcha and sift it into the bowl.

3. Add 30-40mL of hot water (80°C).

4. Whisk vigorously in a zig-zag motion for 30 seconds. Keep your whisk close to the bottom of the bowl but not touching it, using mainly your wrist rather than your arm.

5. Add 90-120mL of your warmed or foamed milk of choice directly into the bowl or into a glass.

6. Mix in 1 tbsp of your sweetener of choice. You can add more or less according to personal preference.

7. Enjoy!

Finished your matcha? Make sure to clean up properly!
