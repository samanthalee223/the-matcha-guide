Usucha / Thin Tea

Usucha is the most common way to drink matcha and is a great way to discover a matcha powder’s flavour profile. Usucha has a thin consistency and is made in small portions, pairing well with both Western and Japanese desserts. Discover More About Usucha!

Materials and Ingredients:

  • 2g or 1/2 teaspoon of matcha
  • 50-100mL of hot water (80°C or 175°F is recommended for the matcha to foam properly)
  • Chasen/bamboo whisk (recommended) or an electric frother
  • Chawan (tea bowl)
  • Chashaku (tea spoon) or measuring spoon
  • Tea sifter
  • Measuring scale

You do not need all of the tea ceremony utensils to make a good bowl of matcha, but they are recommended! You can also change the matcha to water ratios according to personal taste or matcha quality.

Instructions

1. Soak your whisk in hot water for 30-60 seconds. Be sure to wet the top of the tines as well.

2. Measure out 2g or 1/2 teaspoon of matcha and sift it into the bowl.

3. Pour 50-100mL of 80°C or 175°F water into your tea bowl. Adding more water will make a weaker tea, which is recommended for beginners. 70mL-85mL is a good starting point.

4. Whisk vigorously in a zig-zag motion for 20 seconds or until all of the powder is incorporated. Keep your whisk close to the bottom of the bowl but not touching it, using mainly your wrist rather than your arm. A layer of foam with air bubbles should form on the surface of the matcha.

5. Add ice or drink hot and enjoy!

Finished your matcha? Make sure to clean up properly!
