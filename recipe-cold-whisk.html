Cold-Whisked Matcha Latte

Cold-whisking matcha is a non-traditional technique where the matcha powder is whisked in cold milk for a creamier consistency. It is a great beginner friendly way to start drinking matcha but requires more whisking than traditional methods.

Materials and Ingredients:

  • 3g of matcha
  • 120-160mL of your cold milk of choice
  • Optional: a few ice cubes
  • Optional: 1tbsp of your sweetener of choice (honey, sugar, agave syrup, brown sugar syrup, etc.)
  • Chasen/bamboo whisk (recommended) or an electric frother
  • Chawan (tea bowl)
  • Chashaku (tea spoon) or measuring spoon
  • Tea sifter
  • Measuring scale

You do not need all of the tea ceremony utensils to make a good bowl of matcha, but they are recommended! You can also change the matcha to milk ratios according to personal taste.

Instructions

1. Soak your whisk in hot water for 30-60 seconds. Be sure to wet the top of the tines as well.

2. Measure out 3g of matcha and sift it into the bowl.

3. Add 40-50mL of your milk of choice.

4. Whisk vigorously in a zig-zag motion for 30 seconds. Keep your whisk close to the bottom of the bowl but not touching it, using mainly your wrist rather than your arm.

5. Add 90-120mL of your milk of choice directly into the bowl or into a glass with a few ice cubes.

6. Mix in 1 tbsp of your sweetener of choice. You can add more or less according to personal preference.

7. Enjoy!

Finished your matcha? Make sure to clean up properly!
